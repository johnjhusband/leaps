# $100,000 Global Bullish Portfolio — How Many Securities Can You Hold?

**Date:** 2026-06-07  ·  Basket: top ~1000 companies worldwide by market cap (companiesmarketcap.com),
valued with Brandon's method (see wiki/strategy/valuation-method.md), split 50/50 bullish/bearish.

## The constraint is the list, not the money
Interactive Brokers fractional minimum = **the greater of 0.0001 shares or ~$1.00** per order; quantities
to 4 decimals. So $100,000 ÷ $1 = up to 100,000 theoretical slots — **capital and share price never bind.**
The cap is how many bullish names exist *and* are IBKR-fractional-eligible (US/Canada/Europe; Asian local
listings like .T/.SS/.HK/.NS are **not** fractional-eligible).

## Answer
- Global basket: 1000 names, 992 valued, split **496 bullish / 496 bearish** (median dividing line ≈ −12%, i.e. the basket is broadly fully valued).
- **Bullish half that is IBKR-fractional-eligible: 332 securities** — equal-weight ≈ **$301 each**.
- If you require *strictly undervalued* (price below intrinsic, Brandon's real buy rule): 424 global, of which **281 are IBKR-eligible** — equal-weight ≈ **$356 each**.
- The 164 bullish names you *cannot* hold fractionally at IBKR are Asian/other local listings (Japan 36, China/HK 67, India 21, …).

## Bottom line
**You could hold roughly 332 individual securities** (the IBKR-eligible bullish half), or about **281** if you stick to strictly-undervalued names — at $250–$400 each. Fewer, larger positions are fine too; fractional shares just remove the price barrier so the *number* is your choice up to ~332.
