# Exit / Profit-Taking

**Resolved rule (CORRECTED 2026-06-07 — it's instrument-specific, not one rule):**
- **Sold puts:** close around **70–95%** profit, gated by valuation + whether buying power is needed for a more compelling setup (close at ~95% regardless — little juice left; hold at 50% if still deep below intrinsic). The real metric is **profit captured per month held** (he closed ~70–75% after only 4 of 24 months).
- **Bought shares & bought calls:** **no % threshold at all** — exit only when **the story changes / it's meaningfully overvalued** (*"nothing to do with percent profit and everything to do with fundamental value"*). "The market does not know your basis."
- **Trim order:** calls first, then sold puts, then shares last (reverse of the bullish hierarchy).
My earlier flat "~70%, squeeze last 30%" only loosely fits sold puts and was wrong for shares/calls.
