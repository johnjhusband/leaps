# Contradictions & "Traps" — Resolved

The channel repeatedly brands popular strategies as "traps" and says "only 1 strategy works." Those
claims are marketing-framed but internally consistent once you read them as *why Brandon rejects each
in favor of portfolio-secured long-dated puts*. Resolutions below so the spec doesn't average noise.

## "Only 1 options strategy works"
Resolution: the "1 strategy" = **portfolio-secured (not cash-secured) ~2-year puts on undervalued
quality names/indexes, premium recycled into shares + barely-OTM 2-year calls, sized by
assignment-value ratios.** All the "traps" are rejected because each violates one pillar of that.
[[1Ct_EGrYBZU]] [[jWP7cH9Ikpk]]

## "Cash-secured puts are a trap"
Not wrong mechanically — rejected because parking cash as collateral **drags returns**; the same puts
sold *portfolio-secured* keep the collateral invested and capturing market upside. CSP is the
beginner default; PSP is the upgrade. [[B8dFMZfOV6I]] [[1Ct_EGrYBZU]] [[U3je6tIQzYQ]]

## "Covered calls are a trap" — CORRECTED: not a flat trap
**Earlier error (fixed 2026-06-07):** I listed covered calls as a flat reject. They are NOT. Brandon
**sells calls deliberately when a name/market is stretched above the EPS line** — *"I'm going to sell them
those call options for top dollar… generate cash flow when the market's getting expensive"* — ranks
covered calls as a usable strategy, and uses them to exit assigned shares when premium > margin interest.
What he actually **bans is naked calls** (*"I will never sell naked calls"*). The real trap is **mindless
wheel-style covered-call writing regardless of valuation**, and capping upside on a name you're bullish on
(if bearish, just sell the shares). So: selling calls = sanctioned *overvalued-market / bearish* tactic;
trap = doing it on cheap stock. [[j4LK7SI2Kn8]] [[5upcZf1sEJ0]] [[JXdSkRGoiXE]] [[f_r9b1XD-MI]]

## "The wheel is a trap"
Rejected because it's typically run **short-duration on high-beta/meme names**, which get crushed in
drawdowns (down 50% while Nasdaq is down 11%). Duration + quality + ratios are what the wheel lacks. [[crkkAi-nwzI]] [[HOmunMw1Fvw]] [[J930c1BgrnU]]

## "Spreads / credit spreads are a trap"
Rejected because they **cap the profit too low** ("you're making a hundred bucks") for the risk; an
uncapped long-dated PSP collects far more. [[VKnGRB016o8]]

## "Poor man's covered call is a trap"
Grouped with the above as a capped, short-duration substitute that underperforms the PSP+calls book. [[1Ct_EGrYBZU]] [[-3fgTkSJs2E]]

## Theta objection ("you get no theta on a 2-year contract")
Acknowledged and dismissed: you trade theta for **probability and EPS-growth runway** — one 2-year put
at $30k beats needing ~12 monthly wins in a row at $2k each. [[32pMRko-uHA]] [[56GQISel_iY]]
